6.1 Automated Systems

How sensors, microprocessors and actuators work together automatically.

What is an Automated System?

An automated system combines software and hardware to operate without human intervention. Most include a human supervisor for monitoring and override.

Three Key Components

Sensors
Read physical properties (temperature, pressure, light…). Analogue data converted via ADC before reaching the microprocessor.
Microprocessor
Receives sensor data, compares against stored values, decides what action to take.
Actuators
Motors, valves, pumps and pistons that carry out the physical action on receipt of microprocessor signals.

The Loop

1

Sense: sensors detect changes and send data (ADC converts if analogue).

2

Process: microprocessor compares to stored values and decides action.

3

Act: signals sent to actuators.

4

Repeat: continuously without human intervention.

Generic Advantages & Disadvantages

Advantages

  • Faster response than humans
  • Safer in hazardous environments
  • Runs at optimum conditions
  • Cheaper long-term
  • Higher productivity
  • Operates 24/7

Disadvantages

  • Expensive to set up
  • Needs considerable testing
  • Unforeseen situations can be dangerous
  • Vulnerable to cyberattacks
  • Expensive maintenance
  • May cause unemployment

Automated System Examples

Real-world applications from the syllabus.

Industrial

Nuclear Power Station

A Distributed Control System (DCS) monitors sensors for temperature, pressure, flow and radiation. If readings fall outside safe limits, actuators operate pumps, valves or trigger emergency shutdown. Human supervisor can override at any time.

Paracetamol Manufacture

Two simultaneous processes — synthesis and tablet pressing. Sensors feed data to central computer; actuators control pumps, heaters, stirrers and pistons.

Transport

Self-Parking Cars

Sensors and cameras measure parking space as the driver passes. Computer alerts driver when suitable space found. Actuators then control steering, brakes and throttle.

Adaptive Cruise Control

Lasers measure distance to vehicle ahead. Too close → actuators brake/reduce throttle. Safe distance and slow → increase throttle. Driver sets target speed.

Agriculture & Science

Irrigation System

Ultrasonic level sensors send data every 10 minutes. Controller cross-references weather station data; activates water pumps via actuators. Remote supervisor can override.

Automated Weather Station

Thermometer, anemometer (wind), hygrometer (humidity), barometer (pressure), level sensor (rainfall), light sensor (daylight). Data stored automatically to a central database.

Pharmaceutical Lab

Level sensors and colorimeter monitor experiment. Microprocessor controls a tap via actuator — multiple experiments run simultaneously with one supervisor.

6.2 Robotics

Design, construction and operation of robots.

Three Characteristics of a Robot

1

Sense surroundings — sensors detect the environment; data sent to microprocessor.

2

Degree of movement — wheels, cogs, pistons, gears, motors. Interchangeable end effectors (welding torch, gripper, spray gun).

3

Programmable controller — determines actions. Can be reprogrammed for different tasks.

—

Many robots do not have AI — they perform fixed repetitive tasks. Chatbots and web crawlers are software programs, not robots — they don't meet all three characteristics.

Independent vs Dependent

Independent (Autonomous)

  • No direct human control
  • Fully replace human activity
  • Examples: autonomous cars, drones, floor sweepers

Dependent

  • Human interfaces directly
  • Supplements human activity
  • Examples: car assembly where robots and humans work together

Uses of Robots

Industry, transport, agriculture, medicine, domestic, entertainment.

Industry

Programmed by: (1) pre-written instruction sequences, or (2) human demonstrating the task — movements recorded and replayed.

Advantages

  • Work in hazardous conditions
  • Operate 24/7
  • More consistent output
  • Better at repetitive tasks
  • Lower operating costs long-term

Disadvantages

  • Struggle with non-standard tasks
  • Causes unemployment
  • Risk of deskilling workforce
  • High initial cost

Autonomous Vehicles

Cameras, radar, ultrasonic sensors, microprocessors and actuators. Radar/ultrasonic build a 3D image in poor visibility.

Advantages

  • Safer — removes human error
  • Better for environment
  • Reduced congestion
  • ~100% lane capacity increase

Disadvantages

  • Very expensive
  • Risk of hacking
  • Sensors fail in blizzards
  • Driver reluctance
  • Taxi-driver unemployment
—

Autonomous trains use LiDAR (laser-based 3D imaging), proximity sensors on doors, infrared cameras and GPS positioning.

Agriculture, Medicine & Domestic

  • Agriculture: harvesting (vegebot), weed control, phenotyping (spectral/thermal sensors), seed-planting drones, autonomous tractors
  • Medicine: robotic surgery (high precision), prosthetic limbs, automated drug dispensing
  • Domestic: floor sweepers, lawn mowers, window cleaners, pool cleaners
  • Entertainment: friend robots, theme park animatronics, filming drones

6.3 Artificial Intelligence

Simulating intelligent human behaviour in computers.

What is AI?

Artificial Intelligence is the simulation of intelligent human behaviour by a computer — replicating cognitive functions: reasoning, learning, adapting, pattern recognition.

—

AI is a collection of rules and data + the ability to reason, learn and adapt to external stimuli.

Three Categories

Narrow AI
Superior to humans at one specific task. e.g. chess engine beating world champions.
General AI
Performs similarly to humans at a specific task.
Strong AI
Superior to humans across many tasks.

Examples in Use

  • Smart assistants — Alexa, Siri, Google Now: recognise voice commands, learn over time
  • Chatbots — replicate human conversation patterns
  • Autonomous cars — real-time decision making
  • Facial expression recognition — identifies emotional states from landmark positions
  • News generation — automated articles from live data feeds
—

The Turing Test measures whether a machine's responses are indistinguishable from a human's.

6.4 Expert Systems

AI that mimics the knowledge and reasoning of human experts.

Components

User Interface
Dialogue-based interaction; typically Yes/No questions based on previous answers.
Inference Engine
Main processing element. Searches knowledge base, applies inference rules, draws conclusions. The "problem-solving" core.
Knowledge Base
Repository of facts about the domain — objects and their attributes.
Rules Base
IF-THEN inference rules used by the engine to draw conclusions.
Explanation System
Tells users the reasoning behind conclusions so they can judge validity.

Example Inference Rule

IF continent = "South America" AND language = "Portuguese"
THEN country = "Brazil"

Applications & Trade-offs

Used in: oil/mineral prospecting, medical diagnosis, equipment fault diagnostics, tax calculations, chess strategy, logistics routing.

Advantages

  • High expertise and accuracy
  • Stores vast amounts of facts
  • Very fast responses
  • Unbiased analysis
  • Indicates probability of correctness

Disadvantages

  • Requires considerable user training
  • High setup and maintenance costs
  • "Cold" responses (problematic in medicine)
  • Only as good as data entered
  • Users may assume infallibility

6.5 Machine Learning

Algorithms that train on data to make predictions without being explicitly programmed.

What is Machine Learning?

Machine learning is a sub-set of AI where algorithms are trained on data and can make predictions or decisions on new data — without being specifically programmed for each new scenario.

AI (broader)

  • Simulates human intelligence
  • Aims to think like humans
  • Includes machine learning

Machine Learning (sub-set)

  • Trains algorithms on data
  • Makes decisions without explicit programming
  • Learns through data acquisition

Model Development Process

1

Data collection — surveys, web scraping, transaction records.

2

Data cleaning — remove redundant or irrelevant data.

3

Exploration — examine cleaned data for patterns.

4

Build model — train algorithm on real example data.

5

Evaluate — test with known outcomes; adjust as needed.

Examples

Spam Email Detection

Algorithm trained on known spam. Learns patterns: certain phrases, sender behaviour, header anomalies. Applied to new incoming emails to classify as spam or not.

Recommendation Systems

Collaboration filtering: if Customer A bought X, Y, Z and Customer B bought X and Y — recommend Z to Customer B. Powers Amazon, Netflix, Spotify.

Fraud Detection

Trained on normal purchasing behaviour. Unusual activity (large purchase in foreign country) → fraud flagged or blocked.

Search Engines

Learn from user behaviour. Answer found on page 1 → success. User goes to page 3 → failure. Rankings improve over time.

Chapter 6 — Summary

All key emerging technology concepts at a glance.

Automated system

Sensor → ADC → Microprocessor → Actuator. Runs without human intervention.

Control loop

Sense → process → act → repeat. Actuator output feeds back into next sensor reading.

Robot characteristics

Must: (1) sense surroundings, (2) have degree of movement, (3) be programmable.

Robot types

Independent (autonomous, replace humans) vs Dependent (supplement, human oversight).

Autonomous vehicles

Cameras + radar + ultrasonic + GPS + microprocessors + actuators. Safer but expensive; sensors fail in extreme conditions.

Agricultural robots

Harvesting, weed control, phenotyping, seed-planting drones, autonomous tractors.

AI

Simulates cognitive functions — reasoning, learning, adapting, pattern recognition. Narrow / General / Strong.

Expert systems

Knowledge base + rules base + inference engine + user interface + explanation system.

Machine learning

Sub-set of AI. Trains on data → predicts on new data. Spam, recommendations, fraud, search.

Glossary

Key terms from Chapter 6.

Automated systemHardware and software (sensors, microprocessors, actuators) operating automatically without human intervention.
ActuatorAn output device (motor, valve, pump) converting microprocessor signals into physical actions.
Feedback loopIn a control system, actuator output affects the next sensor reading, enabling continuous automatic adjustment.
RoboticsThe branch of computer science dealing with the design, construction and operation of robots.
End effectorThe interchangeable tool at the end of a robot arm (welding torch, spray gun, gripper).
LiDARLight Detection and Ranging — laser pulses building a 3D image of surroundings. Used in autonomous trains and vehicles.
Artificial IntelligenceSimulation of intelligent human behaviour — reasoning, learning, adapting — by a computer system.
Turing TestA test of whether a machine's responses are indistinguishable from a human's.
Expert systemAI that mimics human expert knowledge to solve problems in a specific domain.
Inference engineThe processing component of an expert system that applies rules to the knowledge base to draw conclusions.
Knowledge baseRepository of domain facts used by an expert system's inference engine.
Rules baseA set of IF-THEN inference rules used to draw conclusions in an expert system.
Machine learningA sub-set of AI where algorithms trained on data make predictions on new data without explicit programming.
Collaboration filteringML technique recommending items based on behaviour of users with similar profiles.
